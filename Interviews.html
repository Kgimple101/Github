Interviews

Interviews conducted with users to gain information about the ways this website will be used.

Interview 1: Alyssa Johnson

Alyssa Johnson is a relative of someone with Down Syndrome. She is a user of this website.

1. What information are you looking for most often on this website?
I was mostly trying to locate information regarding the various social activities offered for young adults with special needs and disabilities.

2. What do you enjoy about the website?
It was straightforward with the content and relatively easy to navigate from my mobile device.

3. What frustrates you on the website?
I wouldn’t say I was frustrated about anything. That being said, the only thing that immediately comes to mind is the demographic mentioned: “young adults with disabilities”. Are you primarily referring to physical or intellectual disabilities? Or both? I’m only curious, is all. Perhaps that could be better clarified in your mission statement if your aim is to be inclusive of all individuals. Also, I think it would be helpful to provide an environment where “social outlets” as you’ve described could be enjoyed by all individuals with disabilities- both young children and adults. However, if the target demographic that you’re passionate about connecting with here is specifically young adults then I definitely understand!

Interview 2: Jeni Zapor

Jeni Zapor is a graphic designer but she also volunteers in the youth group at a local church and has taught in schools as well.
She interacts with disabled students regularly and is seeking activities and communities for them.

1. What information are you looking for most often on this website?
I most often look for activities that my adult child with disabilities can participate in.

2. What do you enjoy about the website?
What I liked most were the color choices and the simplicity of the web design. It didn’t seem complicated to use.

3. What frustrates you on the website?
What I found frustrating was when I went to the Donate Services section and tried to read about it, the script kept jumping with the different sizes of those who donated. I never finished reading because it was super irritating.

Interview 3: Susan Pitcher

Susan Pitcher is an administrator at Cane Bay High School. She interacts with and oversees
disabled students daily and is seeking activities and communities for them.

1. What information are you looking for most often on this website?
Testimonials and year established. I really had to look more into the links to get an understanding of what The Exceptional was promoting. Mission and welcome should be on the main.

2. What do you enjoy about the website?
Easy to navigate and nice graphics. Loved the gallery. Gave me a up front closer look at what the mission looks like physically.
